Orthognathic Surgery

Harmonizing Function and Aesthetics

Orthognathic surgery, or corrective jaw surgery, addresses skeletal irregularities of the jawbones. It is a powerful procedure for patients whose bite problems cannot be resolved with orthodontics alone. Conditions such as an underbite, overbite, open bite, or facial asymmetry can lead to difficulties with chewing, speech, and even breathing, as well as chronic jaw pain.

The surgery involves strategically repositioning the upper jaw, lower jaw, or both, to create a correct and stable bite. While the primary goal is functional improvement, the procedure often results in a significant enhancement of facial harmony and aesthetics, boosting patient confidence.

The Process: From Planning to Recovery

The journey is a collaborative effort, typically involving close coordination with your orthodontist before and after the procedure.

Step 1: Consultation & Virtual Surgical Planning (VSP)

Using 3D scans and advanced software, we perform the entire surgery virtually before ever entering the operating room. This allows for unparalleled precision in planning the exact movements of the jawbones and helps you visualize the final outcome.

Step 2: The Surgical Procedure

Performed under general anesthesia, most incisions are made inside the mouth to avoid facial scars. The jawbones are carefully moved to their new, planned position and secured with small titanium plates and screws, which integrate with the bone and do not need to be removed.

Step 3: Recovery & Final Orthodontics

Initial healing takes about six weeks, during which a modified diet is required. Swelling is normal and gradually subsides. After the jaws have healed, your orthodontist will perform the final teeth alignment to perfect your new bite.
