Facial Trauma Surgery

Expert Management of Facial Injuries

Facial trauma, also called maxillofacial trauma, refers to any physical injury to the face. This can range from relatively minor cuts and bruises to severe, life-altering fractures of the facial bones, including the jaw (mandible), cheekbones (zygoma), nose, and the delicate bones surrounding the eyes (orbits). As a maxillofacial surgeon, Dr. Bandyopadhyay's unique training provides the expertise to manage these complex injuries, addressing not only the physical damage but also the critical functional and aesthetic implications for the patient.

Improperly treated facial fractures can lead to long-term issues with vision, breathing, chewing, and speaking, as well as significant cosmetic deformities. Our primary goals are to restore the patient's facial structure, re-establish proper function, and achieve the best possible cosmetic outcome with minimal scarring.

The Treatment Process

Treatment for facial trauma is highly individualized, depending on the severity and location of the injury. The process is focused on timely and precise intervention to ensure the best possible recovery and prevent long-term complications.

Step 1: Emergency Assessment and Diagnosis

Upon arrival at the trauma center, a comprehensive assessment is performed to evaluate the extent of the injuries. Advanced imaging, such as a 3D CT scan, is the gold standard. It provides a detailed, multi-angled view of the facial skeleton, allowing for the precise diagnosis of all fractures and the creation of a meticulous surgical plan.

Step 2: Surgical Repair (ORIF)

Most facial fracture repairs involve a procedure called Open Reduction and Internal Fixation (ORIF). "Open reduction" means the fractured bones are accessed and carefully repositioned into their correct, pre-injury alignment. "Internal fixation" refers to securing the bones in place using small, strong, biocompatible titanium plates and screws. These are designed to be permanent and do not typically require removal. To minimize visible scarring, incisions are strategically placed inside the mouth, in the hairline, or along natural skin creases whenever possible.
