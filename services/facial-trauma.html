Facial Trauma Surgery

What is Facial Trauma Surgery?

[PLACEHOLDER] Facial trauma, also called maxillofacial trauma, is any physical injury to the face. This can range from minor cuts and bruises to severe fractures of the facial bones, including the jaw (mandible), cheekbones (zygoma), nose, and eye sockets (orbits). As a maxillofacial surgeon, Dr. Bandyopadhyay is uniquely qualified to manage these injuries, addressing not only the physical damage but also the functional and aesthetic implications.

[PLACEHOLDER] The primary goals of facial trauma surgery are to restore the patient's facial structure, re-establish proper function of the jaws and vision, and achieve the best possible cosmetic outcome. This requires a deep understanding of facial anatomy and a meticulous surgical approach.

Key Benefits of Expert Care

  • Accurate repair of bone fractures for proper healing
  • Restoration of a normal bite and ability to chew
  • Preservation of vision and eye function in orbital injuries
  • Minimal scarring through careful incision placement
  • Return to a normal facial appearance and symmetry

The Treatment Process

[PLACEHOLDER] Treatment for facial trauma is highly individualized and depends on the severity and location of the injury. The process is focused on timely and precise intervention to ensure the best possible recovery.

Step 1: Emergency Assessment and Diagnosis

[PLACEHOLDER] Upon arrival, a comprehensive assessment is performed. Advanced imaging, such as a 3D CT scan, is crucial to accurately diagnose all fractures and soft tissue injuries. This allows for the creation of a detailed surgical plan.

Step 2: The Surgical Repair

[PLACEHOLDER] Surgery often involves open reduction and internal fixation (ORIF), where the fractured bones are carefully repositioned into their correct alignment. They are then secured in place using small, strong titanium plates and screws. Whenever possible, incisions are made inside the mouth or along natural skin creases to minimize visible scarring.

Step 3: Recovery and Rehabilitation

[PLACEHOLDER] The recovery period varies based on the extent of the injuries. Post-operative care will focus on managing swelling and pain, and may include a temporary modified diet to allow the jaws to heal. Follow-up appointments are essential to monitor the healing process and ensure that function is fully restored.
