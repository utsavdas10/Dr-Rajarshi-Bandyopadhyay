Area of Expertise

TMJ (Jaw Joint) Surgeries

What are TMJ Disorders?

[PLACEHOLDER] The Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ) is the joint that connects your jawbone to your skull. TMJ disorders (TMD) are a group of conditions that cause pain and dysfunction in the jaw joint and the muscles that control jaw movement. While many cases can be managed with non-surgical treatments, some patients with severe or persistent problems may require surgery.

[PLACEHOLDER] Dr. Bandyopadhyay is an expert in the full spectrum of TMJ management, from minimally invasive procedures to total joint replacement. The goal of surgery is to relieve pain, restore normal jaw function, and improve the patient's quality of life.

When is TMJ Surgery Considered?

  • When conservative treatments (like physical therapy, bite guards) have failed
  • In cases of severe, chronic pain that impacts daily life
  • For specific structural problems within the joint, like a displaced disc
  • In patients with degenerative joint disease, such as osteoarthritis or rheumatoid arthritis
  • For conditions like ankylosis (fusion of the joint) that severely restrict mouth opening

Types of TMJ Surgery

[PLACEHOLDER] The type of surgery recommended depends entirely on the specific diagnosis. The approach ranges from minimally invasive to complex open-joint procedures.

Arthrocentesis & Arthroscopy (Minimally Invasive)

[PLACEHOLDER] Arthrocentesis is a simple procedure to flush out inflammatory substances from the joint. TMJ arthroscopy uses a tiny camera (arthroscope) inserted into the joint through a small incision. This allows the surgeon to see inside the joint, remove inflamed tissue, and reposition a displaced disc.

Open-Joint Surgery (Arthroplasty)

[PLACEHOLDER] For more advanced issues, an open-joint procedure may be necessary. This involves a larger incision to directly access and repair or remove damaged tissue within the joint. This may be done to treat bony growths, scarring, or to repair the disc.

Total Joint Replacement

[PLACEHOLDER] In cases of severe degeneration or end-stage TMJ disease, much like a hip or knee replacement, the damaged joint can be replaced with a custom-made artificial prosthesis. This is a highly effective procedure for restoring function and eliminating pain in patients with the most advanced conditions.
