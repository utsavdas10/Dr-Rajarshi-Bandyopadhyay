Oral Oncology (Head & Neck Cancer)

Comprehensive & Compassionate Cancer Care

Oral oncology is a subspecialty focused on the diagnosis, surgical treatment, and complex reconstruction of cancers affecting the head and neck. This includes cancers of the mouth (oral cavity), jaws, tongue, salivary glands, and surrounding structures. The primary goals of oncologic surgery are twofold: first, the complete surgical removal of the tumor with clear margins to achieve a cure, and second, the immediate reconstruction of the affected areas to preserve quality of life.

Dr. Bandyopadhyay is a key member of a multidisciplinary team, working closely with medical oncologists, radiation oncologists, pathologists, and rehabilitation specialists to provide integrated, state-of-the-art care.

The Patient's Journey

A cancer diagnosis can be overwhelming. Our approach is to guide you through every step of the process with expertise, clarity, and compassion.

Step 1: Diagnosis and Multidisciplinary Planning

The journey begins with a definitive diagnosis, typically confirmed via a biopsy. Your case will then be presented and discussed in a multidisciplinary tumor board. This collaborative meeting ensures that experts from every relevant specialty contribute to creating the most effective treatment plan tailored to your specific cancer type, stage, and overall health.

Step 2: Surgical Resection and Reconstruction

The surgical phase often involves two main parts performed in a single, extensive operation. First, the cancerous tumor is meticulously removed along with a safety margin of healthy tissue. Second, the defect created by the tumor removal is immediately reconstructed. This frequently involves microvascular free flap surgery, where tissue (skin, muscle, and/or bone) is transferred from another part of the body (like the leg or forearm) to rebuild the jaw, tongue, or cheek. The tiny blood vessels of the flap are then painstakingly connected to vessels in the neck under a microscope to ensure the transferred tissue lives and heals.
